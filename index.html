Core Research Agenda & Technical Foundation

Rather than analyzing retroactive case law, my research focus is predictive and stress-test-driven, concentrating on three primary pillars:

  • Comparative Architectures of Cyber-Governance & Sovereign Tech: Conducting comparative, multijurisdictional analyses of primary statutory sources, national security mandates, and regulatory baselines across leading global technological blocks. This focus involves executing functional legal assessments to identify regulatory lacunae in international conventions, stress-testing legal frameworks against emerging cyberthreats, and deconstructing their divergent regulatory approaches.
  • Liability Allocation in Decentralized Architectures: Analyzing regulatory friction and the imputation of liability under the Revised Product Liability Directive (PLD) concerning distributed processing systems, specifically Federated Machine Learning.
  • Gaming Ecosystem Governance & Anti-Cheat Policies: Investigating the legal boundaries of aggressive kernel-level surveillance and digital platform Terms of Service (ToS).
